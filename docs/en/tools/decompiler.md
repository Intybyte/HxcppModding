Ghidra, IDA, Binary Ninja any decompiler is good.

I personally use Ghidra.

Do your own research and experimentation on which one you are more familiar with. Rarely you
are going to share directly with other people your decompiling efforts. And if you are, it is
probably not going to be sharing file type of stuff, but sad screenshots of decompiled stuff. 

### Cons

If you have long methods and a bad PC, Ghidra will choke.

IDA is expensive.

Binary Ninja has less aggresive analysis, also more features are paid.

### Pros

Ghidra is free, widely available and has an huge ecosystem.

IDA seems to give the most accurate output

Binary Ninja has a cleaner and more modern output
